Gao, Fengnan

Advanced Statistics in 2018/19 Spring Semester

Basic Info

  • Location: H5114. Time: every Wednesday and Friday from 18:30 - 21:00, from Week 2 (March 4) to Week 9 (April 26), except for April 5.
  • The course provides a comprehensive review on the classical topics of asymptotic statistics.
  • Prerequisites: real analysis and linear algebra, basic probability and statistics.
  • There is no homework for this course. However, each student is required to scribe a certain part of the lecture notes. The exact arrangement depends on the number of students taking part in the course.
  • The students will be assigned to teams to choose from a list of papers and/or chapters of books to read and present the results.

Course Contents

The course mostly covers the following contents:
  1. Preliminaries
    1. Notations
    2. Delta Method
    3. Likelihood function
  2. Sufficiency and Ancillary
    1. Sufficient statistics
    2. Minimal sufficient statistics
    3. Completeness and minimal sufficiency
    4. Ancillarity
  3. Unbiased point estimation
    1. Unbiased point estimator
    2. The Cramér–Rao lower bound
  4. Method of Moments and Maximum Likelihood Estimation
    1. Method of moment estimation
    2. Maximum Likelihood Estimation - Computation and properties
  5. General theory for M-estimation
    1. General framework
    2. Consistency
    3. Asymptotic normality
  6. Efficiency lower bound: Asymptotic theory
    1. Hodge's estimator and super-efficiency
    2. Le Cam's contiguity theory
    3. Convergence to normal modes
    4. Convolution theorem
  7. Hypothesis testing
    1. MP and UMP tests
    2. Likelihood ratio test
    3. Efficiency of tests
    4. Pitman's relative efficiency
    5. Confidence interval
  8. Bayesian Inference
    1. Conjugate priors
    2. Bayesian point and interval estimation
    3. Bernstein-von Mises theorem and its proof
  9. Elementary Decision theory
    1. Risk: the frequentist approach
    2. Bayes' risk
    3. Bayes' rule and admissibility
    4. Admissibility and inadmissibility in Gaussian location models

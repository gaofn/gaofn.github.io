Course Overview

  • The course is modeled after Qiwei Yao's course.
  • The course will provide a comprehensive coverage on some fundamental aspects of probability and statistics methods and principles. It also covers linear regression analysis. Data illustration using statistical package R constitutes an integral part throughout the course, therefore provides the hands-on experience in simulation and data analysis.
  • You may download the syllabus (in Chinese) here and (in English) here.
  • It includes the following chapters:
    1. Introduction to R: What is R? Installing R, help and documentation, data objects, data import and export, basic data manipulation, computing with data, organising an analysis.
    2. Probability: sample space and events, probability, independent events, conditional probability, Bayes’ formula.
    3. Random variables and distributions: distribution functions and probability functions, mean and variance, moment generating functions, discrete random variables, continuous random variables.
    4. Multivariate distributions: bivariate distributions, marginal distributions, independent random variables, conditional distributions, multivariate distributions, IID samples, transformations of random variables.
    5. Inequalities: probability inequalities, inequalities for expectations.
    6. Convergence of random variables and Monte Carlo Methods: types of convergence, law of large numbers (LLN), central limit theorem (CLT), Monte Carlo integrals, importance sampling.
    7. Introduction to Statistical Inference: what is statistics? parametric and nonparametric models, fundamental concepts in inference, empirical distributions.
    8. Bootstrap: simulation, bootstrap variance estimation, bootstrap confidence intervals.
    9. Point estimation: method of moments estimation, maximum likelihood estimation (MLE), properties of MLE, asymptotic efficiency, parametric bootstrap, EM algorithms.
    10. Hypothesis testing I: null and alternative hypotheses, p-values, two-types of errors, the Wald test, t-tests and t-intervals.
    11. Hypothesis testing II: likelihood ratio tests, Pearson’s chi-square-test, goodness-of-fit tests, permutation tests.
    12. Regression models: simple linear regression, least squares estimation (LSE), prediction, multiple linear regression, model selection.
